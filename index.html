Premium hunts

A separate draw for one bonus deer, elk, or pronghorn tag with a 4-month season (Aug 1 – Nov 30), either-sex, any weapon. No points involved — everyone has the same random shot. One tag per unit, and it's an extra tag on top of your regular one.

Odds below are from the most recent premium drawing's applicant counts. Apply by May 15 — $8 per species.

What this app does

It takes Oregon's official controlled-hunt numbers and answers one question in plain English: which deer or elk hunts can you realistically draw, and are they worth your points?

Enter your preference points on the Hunts page, and every hunt gets a color-coded answer. Tap any hunt to see the full story — your odds, hunter success rates, antler sizes, and how the hunt has changed over the years.

Where the numbers come from

Everything comes from the Oregon Department of Fish & Wildlife's public reports. A helper program checks ODFW's website on the 1st of every month and loads any new reports automatically — nobody has to do anything.

Draw reports for a season are published in the fall, after the June drawing. Harvest results are published the following spring.

Words you'll see

Preference points — Every year you apply and don't draw, ODFW gives you a point. More points = better odds next year. Your points are on your ODFW account.

Points for a sure tag — Hunters who had this many points all drew a tag. If you have this many or more, the tag is yours if you apply.

Success rate — Of the hunters who held this tag, the share who brought an animal home.

Luck-of-the-draw — Some hunts never fill up with point-holders, so 25% of tags go out by pure random luck. Anyone can win one.

⚠ next to a success rate — Fewer than 6 in 10 tag holders reported their hunt, so the rate is based on a small slice of hunters and probably reads higher than reality.

Personal reference tool. Not affiliated with ODFW. Always verify hunt numbers, dates, and boundaries in the current Oregon Big Game Regulations before applying. Application deadline is May 15 each year.
